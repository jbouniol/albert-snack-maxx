## 🧭 Context

Snack Maxx has operated 2,000 vending machines across universities and Paris metro stations since 1985. Revenue dropped from €50M (1990) to €25M (2026). Target for 2029: €50M in revenue, €15M in profit. The root cause is not demand: customers are captive on campus or in transit. The problem is an outdated experience: cash only, opaque product selection, zero post-purchase customer relationship.

The Mobile App is the **primary digital touchpoint** between Snack Maxx and its customers. It determines the company's ability to shift from an anonymous transactional model to a recurring, data-driven customer relationship.

---

## 🎯 CEO Customer Objectives: Mobile App

### 1. Make purchasing faster than cash

Customers must be able to buy in under 10 seconds between classes or metro stops, without searching for coins.

**Success metric:** median purchase time via app < 10s (vs ~60s with cash today).

### 2. Turn every machine into an identified touchpoint

Customers are no longer anonymous. The app links every purchase to a profile, enabling Snack Maxx to understand real demand and stop stocking based on supplier guesswork.

**Success metric:** 60% of transactions attributed to a user account within 12 months.

### 3. Give customers the power to influence product selection

Customers can vote for the products they want in their campus or station machine. They feel heard; Snack Maxx stocks what actually sells.

**Success metric:** 30% of active users vote at least once per month.

### 4. Eliminate the "empty machine / broken machine" frustration

Customers know in real time whether their usual machine is stocked and functional before going there. No more disappointment in front of an empty slot.

**Success metric:** NPS on product availability > 50.

### 5. Build a daily habit, not an occasional impulse

The app rewards repetition (loyalty, streaks, personalized offers) to turn impulse purchases into a ritual. Student purchasing power is limited, but frequency can increase.

**Success metric:** average frequency from 2x/week to 4x/week among app users.

### 6. Deliver a perceived price fairer than the kiosk next door

Dynamic pricing, flash offers, bundles, monthly snack subscription: the app must make Snack Maxx competitive despite higher structural costs.

**Success metric:** average perceived price rated "fair or cheap" by 70% of app users.

### 7. Close the feedback loop in real time

Customers can report an issue (missing product, jammed machine, expired item) in 2 taps. Snack Maxx responds and acts. Frustration becomes actionable data.

**Success metric:** < 5% of tickets unresolved within 24h.
